How to Report Your Wait Time

Reports from real travelers are what make this app accurate. If you are at an airport right now, please take 30 seconds to submit your wait time. Here is exactly how:

1

Tap the Report tab

Look at the bottom navigation bar and tap the ➕ icon labeled "Report." This opens the submission form.

2

Use the timer OR enter manually

If you are still in line — tap START TIMER when you join the queue. Tap STOP when you clear security. Your wait time fills in automatically. Or simply type the number of minutes you waited.

3

Select your airport and terminal

Choose your airport from the dropdown. Then select the terminal you went through — e.g. Terminal A, Terminal B, or Main. This is printed on your boarding pass.

4

Select your lane type

Choose which lane you used: Standard (regular security), TSA PreCheck (expedited, no shoes off), CLEAR + PreCheck, or Expedited. This helps other travelers pick the fastest lane for their situation.

5

Tap Submit Report

Hit the blue Submit button. Your report appears instantly in the Live Feed and updates the airport's average wait time for everyone. No account required to submit.

FAQ

Common Questions

Is this app free?

Yes — checking wait times is completely free with no account required. A free account unlocks custom alerts. A Pro plan ($4.99/month) unlocks AI predictions, historical trends, and an ad-free experience.

Do I need to download anything?

No download required. Open tsawaittracker.com in Safari or Chrome on your phone. You can add it to your home screen for instant access — tap the Share button in Safari and select "Add to Home Screen."

How accurate is the data?

Accuracy improves as more travelers submit reports. Each report is weighted by recency — a 5-minute-old report carries more weight than a 55-minute-old one. Reports older than 60 minutes are automatically de-weighted.

My airport is not showing wait times — why?

That airport has no recent reports yet. You can be the first — submit your wait time and it will show immediately. The more travelers who report at that airport, the more accurate it becomes.

What is the difference between the lane types?

Standard is the regular security line for all travelers. TSA PreCheck is expedited screening — no shoes, laptops, or liquids removal ($78 enrollment, 5 years). CLEAR + PreCheck uses biometrics to skip to the front of PreCheck. Expedited varies by airport.

How do I set up an alert for my home airport?

Create a free account, tap the Alerts tab, select your airport, set your threshold in minutes, and tap Add Alert. You will receive a push notification when the average wait exceeds your limit.

What airports are covered?

We currently track 70+ airports across all 50 U.S. states — from major hubs like ATL, LAX, ORD, and JFK to regional airports including OKC, TUL, BNA, and more. We are adding airports continuously based on user requests.
